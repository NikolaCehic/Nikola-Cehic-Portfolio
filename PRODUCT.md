# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Senior engineering recruiters, founders, technical hiring managers, and product or protocol leaders evaluating Nikola Cehic for senior fullstack, frontend-heavy fullstack, Web3 product engineering, crypto infrastructure, and AI-native product roles. They arrive with limited time, often from LinkedIn, GitHub, a CV link, or a direct referral, and need fast evidence of seniority, ownership, technical range, and product judgment.

## Product Purpose

The portfolio is a high-signal personal brand site that turns a dense engineering history into a clear hiring narrative. It should prove that Nikola can own production systems across frontend, backend, data, and crypto infrastructure, while still shipping polished product surfaces. Success means the visitor quickly understands the fit, trusts the depth, sees concrete shipped work, and has an obvious path to contact or download the CV.

## Brand Personality

Precise, infrastructural, and cinematic. The voice is senior and direct: confident without hype, technical without costume, polished without feeling like a generic SaaS template.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic terminal-only hacker portfolios, beige editorial tech landing pages, crypto-neon spectacle, identical card grids, fake metrics, stock startup illustrations, and decorative effects that hide the actual work. The site should not feel like a theme demo, a CV pasted into cards, or an AI-generated landing page with Web3 vocabulary sprinkled over it.

## Design Principles

1. Lead with proof of ownership: every major section should answer what was owned, shipped, and operated in production.
2. Make complexity legible: crypto, indexers, APIs, data models, and design systems should be expressed as clear systems, not unexplained jargon.
3. Use design as evidence: the site itself should demonstrate product taste, motion restraint, responsive care, accessibility, and implementation quality.
4. Preserve senior density: let the page be information-rich, but organize it so a busy evaluator can scan quickly.
5. Keep the contact path obvious: every high-intent visitor should know how to start a conversation or get the CV.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast and keyboard accessibility. Respect reduced motion, avoid hover-only functionality, keep headings and landmarks semantic, maintain readable line lengths, and ensure contact/CV actions remain reachable on mobile and desktop.
